Filipino-style Picadillo

What is Picadllo

An influence of Spanish colonization, picadillo is a dish popular in the Philippines as well as other Latin countries. Its name is from the Spanish word “picar” which means “to mince.” It is traditionally made of ground or minced beef, fresh tomatoes or tomato sauce, and other ingredients abundant in the region such as potatoes, carrots, green peas, olives, and capers.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 small onion, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cloves garlics, peeled and minced
  • 1 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup water or beef broth
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 mediium carrots, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup of frozen green peas, thawed
  • 1/4 cup green olivers, pitted
  • salt and pepper to taste

Prepartion

  1. In a pan over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic and cook until softened
  2. Add ground beef and cook, breaking to pieces with the back of a spoon, until lightly browned. Drain excess fat, if any.
  3. Add fish sauce and cook for about 1 to 2 minutes.
  4. Add tomato sauce and crushed tomatoes
  5. Add water and bring to boil. Lower heat, cover and cook for about 10 to 15 minutes or until beef is cooked through.
  6. Add potatoes and carrots. Cover and continue to simmer for about 3 to 5 minutes or until tender.
  7. Add green peas and olives
  8. Continue to cook until sauce is reduced to thickened. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
